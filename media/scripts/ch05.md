## Segment 1 - Rank tells product from entangled (scene: `EntanglementRankScene`, ~0:00-0:40)

Two subsystems combine by the tensor product, not the Cartesian product. A joint
state that factors as a-tensor-b describes two independent parts. A state that does
not factor is entangled - Schrodinger called it the characteristic trait of quantum
mechanics.

There is a clean test. Write the joint state's coefficients as a matrix. A product
state's coefficient matrix is a single outer product, so it has rank one.

[cue: the product state's coefficient matrix appears, rank one]

The Bell state's coefficient matrix is the identity, rank two.

[cue: the Bell state's matrix appears beside it, rank two]

A rank-two matrix is not a single outer product, so the Bell state cannot be
written as a-tensor-c. That is the whole proof: match coefficients, and you are
forced into a contradiction. The excess rank - the Schmidt rank - is exactly the
amount of entanglement, and it is the resource behind Bell-inequality violation and
the power of quantum computation.
