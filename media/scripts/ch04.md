## Segment 1 - Evolution preserves length (scene: `UnitaryConservesNormScene`, ~0:00-0:35)

A closed system evolves in time by a unitary operator. In finite dimensions a
unitary is exactly a linear map that preserves the inner product, and therefore the
length of the state vector.

[cue: the state vector rotates around the circle, its tip tracing the unit circle,
its length never changing]

Watch the state evolve. It turns, but it never grows or shrinks. That invariance is
not a detail - it is conservation of probability. The total Born probability of a
state is its squared length, so if the length is fixed, the probabilities keep
summing to one at every instant. A normalized state stays normalized forever. The
proof is a single line on top of the Born rule: total probability is squared norm,
and a unitary preserves the norm. The one-parameter family e-to-the-minus-i-t-H is
this evolution; extending it to infinite dimensions, where H is unbounded, is the
work of Stone's theorem and the project's frontier.
